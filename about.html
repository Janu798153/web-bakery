How it began

From a Tiny Kitchen to a Beloved Bakery

It started in 2008 with a wood-fired oven, a 100-year-old sourdough starter, and an unshakeable belief that people deserve better bread. Our founder, Eleanor Crumble, had spent three years apprenticing under bakers in Lyon and San Francisco before returning home with fire in her hands.

The first Saturday market stall sold out in under an hour. By the end of that year, a permanent home was found at 42 Baker Street â€” a space that still smells of caramelised crust and browned butter every morning.

Today, a team of 18 bakers continues that original mission: to bake things that genuinely nourish, that make people pause, that bring neighbours together around a table.
